NASA Goddard Space Flight Center

Supervisor: Lan Jian, PhD
Research AST, Fields and Particles
lan.jian@nasa.gov

Summary: The first part of this internship, Space Weather Forecasting, required a deep knowledge of the subject of space weather. Throughout the 10-week course of this internship, hourly updates were sent during work hours (9-5). These regular updates were important and required great attention to detail. The research portion of this internship involved the assessment of the WSA-ENLIL+Cone model (the leading model for the forecasting of CMEs) during an optimal period of observational spacecraft. Various inputs were used for the simulations of 6 different events and simulation results were compared with real-time results. The internship culminated with the presentation of research and results, done through a poster presentation and written paper.

NASA Ames Research Center

Design Engineer, Construction Engineer October 2017 – February 2018 (5 months)

Supervisor: Ali Guarneros Luna
TechEdSat 8 CSO
ali.guarnerosluna@nasa.gov

Summary: The NCAS internship consisted of two primary phases, the first of which involved a design submission of a new Mars rover. The NCAS team then executed a merit-based selection process in which some interns moved on to the second phase of the program. The second phase was on-location at Ames Research Center and required interns to be put into teams of four, tasked with designing and building a fully-functioning miniature version of the rover. Design engineer involved developing a design for the rover that would function well for the given tasks, and my position as construction engineer required the construction of said design, using available parts and techniques.
